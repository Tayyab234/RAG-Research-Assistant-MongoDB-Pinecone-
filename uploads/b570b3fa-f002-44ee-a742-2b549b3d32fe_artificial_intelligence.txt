## Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence (AI) is the academic field devoted to creating systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as reasoning, learning, perception, and language understanding. It bridges computer science, cognitive psychology, mathematics, and engineering, shaping modern technologies from web search and recommendation systems to autonomous vehicles.

### Key facts

* **Origins:** Formally founded as a field in 1956 at the Dartmouth Conference.
* **Core methods:** Machine learning, knowledge representation, reasoning, and natural language processing.
* **Subfields:** Includes computer vision, robotics, expert systems, and reinforcement learning.
* **Applications:** Used in healthcare, finance, education, transportation, and creative industries.

### Historical background

Early AI research focused on symbolic reasoning and problem solving, influenced by pioneers such as Alan Turing and John McCarthy. After cycles of optimism and setbacks—often called “AI winters”—the field advanced rapidly in the 21st century with improved algorithms, large datasets, and powerful computing hardware.

### Core approaches

AI methods generally fall into two categories: symbolic AI, which manipulates explicit rules and representations, and statistical or machine learning approaches, where systems learn patterns from data. Deep learning, a subset of machine learning using multi-layered neural networks, has driven breakthroughs in image recognition, speech processing, and game-playing agents.

### Societal impact and ethics

AI has become a central force in automation, scientific discovery, and global industry. It also raises ethical questions about bias, privacy, employment, and accountability. Researchers and policymakers are developing frameworks for trustworthy and transparent AI aligned with human values.

### Current research directions

Contemporary AI research explores areas such as explainable AI, general intelligence, and human–AI collaboration. It increasingly emphasizes fairness, interpretability, and sustainable deployment as intelligent systems permeate everyday life and decision-making worldwide.
